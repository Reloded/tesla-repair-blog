Suspension noises in a Tesla are noticeable because the car is so quiet without an engine. What might be hidden by engine noise in a gas car becomes very apparent in an EV. The good news: many suspension noises are simple fixes.

## Types of Suspension Noise

### Clunking

**Sound:** Solid "thunk" or "clunk" over bumps
**Common causes:**
- Worn control arm bushings
- Loose [sway bar end links](/posts/tesla-sway-bar-link-replacement/) â€” one of the most common causes
- Worn ball joints
- Loose wheel lug nuts
- Bad strut mount â€” [worn struts](/posts/tesla-strut-replacement/) can cause clunking

### Squeaking

**Sound:** High-pitched squeak while driving or over bumps
**Common causes:**
- Dry bushings
- Control arm bushings need lubrication
- Dust shields rubbing
- Brake components

### Creaking

**Sound:** Low-pitched creak, especially when turning or at low speed
**Common causes:**
- Strut mount bearing
- Sway bar bushings
- Coil spring seating

### Rattling

**Sound:** Loose or metallic rattle
**Common causes:**
- Heat shield loose
- Loose underbody panels
- Brake hardware
- Items in the car (check trunk, frunk)

## Diagnosing the Problem

### Step 1: Narrow Down Location

- **Front or rear?** Drive over bumps and try to determine which end
- **Left or right?** Turn sharply each directionâ€”noise louder on one side?
- **Over bumps only, or also turning?** Bump noise = shocks/links; turning = bushings/bearings

### Step 2: Visual Inspection

With the car safely on jack stands:

1. **Check for obvious damage** - Torn boots, missing hardware
2. **Inspect bushings** - Look for cracking, dry rot, or damage
3. **Check for loose components** - Grab suspension parts and try to wiggle them
4. **Look for rubbing** - Shiny spots indicate metal-on-metal contact

### Step 3: The Bounce Test

1. Push down firmly on each corner of the car
2. Release and let it bounce
3. The car should settle within 1-2 bounces
4. Excessive bouncing = worn shocks/struts
5. Noise during bouncing = mount or bushing issue

### Step 4: Pry Bar Test

With the car on jack stands:
1. Use a pry bar to apply force to suspension components
2. Check for play in ball joints, tie rod ends, and bushings
3. There should be minimal or no play in these parts

## Common Tesla-Specific Issues

### Model 3/Y Control Arm Bushings

A common complaint is squeaking from the front lower control arm bushings. Tesla has revised these parts multiple times.

**Symptoms:**
- Squeak over small bumps
- Noise worse in cold weather
- Noise when first starting to drive

**DIY Fix:**
1. Locate the lower control arm (front suspension)
2. Apply silicone spray or rubber conditioner to the bushings
3. This is a temporary fixâ€”replacement may be needed eventually

**Permanent Fix:**
- [Control arm replacement](/posts/tesla-control-arm-replacement/) (requires [alignment](/posts/tesla-wheel-alignment-diy/) after)
- ~$200-400 at an independent shop
- ~$600+ at Tesla

### Sway Bar End Links

All Tesla models can develop clunking from worn end links.

**Symptoms:**
- Clunk over bumps, especially one side at a time
- Noise when going from acceleration to braking

**DIY Fix:**
- Sway bar end links are relatively easy to replace
- Cost: $30-80 per side for parts
- Requires basic tools and 30 minutes per side

### Dust Shield Rattle

A thin metal heat/dust shield near the brakes can come loose and rattle.

**Symptoms:**
- Metallic rattle at certain speeds
- Rattle may come and go

**DIY Fix:**
1. Locate the loose shield (usually visible behind the wheel)
2. Bend it away from moving parts, or
3. Use a large hose clamp to secure it, or
4. Remove it entirely (usually not critical)

## DIY Fixes for Common Noises

### Lubricating Squeaky Bushings

For dry rubber bushings that squeak:

1. Clean the area with brake cleaner
2. Apply silicone spray lubricant to the bushing
3. Work the suspension (push down on the car) to distribute
4. Wipe off excess

**Note:** This is a temporary fix. If bushings are cracked or torn, they need replacement.

### Tightening Loose Components

Check these for looseness:
- Lug nuts (should be 129 ft-lbs on Model 3/Y)
- Sway bar end link nuts
- Control arm bolts
- Caliper bracket bolts

### Securing Loose Underbody Panels

Tesla's underbody aero panels can come loose:
1. Inspect all visible clips and fasteners
2. Replace missing or broken clips
3. Tesla clips can be purchased aftermarket
4. Push pins should click securely into place

## When to Seek Professional Help

Some suspension work requires special tools or alignment:

| Job | DIY? | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| Bushing lubrication | Yes | Temporary fix |
| End link replacement | Yes | Basic tools |
| Shock/strut replacement | Maybe | May need spring compressor |
| Control arm replacement | No | Requires alignment after |
| Ball joint replacement | No | Press required, safety critical |
| [Wheel bearing replacement](/posts/tesla-wheel-bearing-replacement/) | No | Special tools required, humming noise may indicate wear |

## Documenting Noises for Service

If you take your car in for service, help the technician by documenting:

1. **When does it happen?** (Speed, temperature, turning, bumps)
2. **Where does it come from?** (Front/rear, left/right)
3. **What does it sound like?** (Clunk, squeak, rattle)
4. **Is it getting worse?**
5. **Record a video** if possible
