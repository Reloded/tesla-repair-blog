## Symptoms of a Failed Coolant Valve

- **VCFRONT_a210** or similar error code on the touchscreen
- Cabin heater blowing cold air
- Reduced Supercharging speed (battery can't thermally precondition)
- "Climate system has a fault" warning
- Unusual coolant pump noises

## Step-by-Step Replacement

### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle

1. Park on a flat, level surface
2. Enable **Jack Mode** (Controls > Service > Jack Mode)
3. Let the car cool down completely â€” coolant can be scalding hot
4. Disconnect the 12V battery as a precaution (see our [12V battery guide](/posts/tesla-12v-battery-replacement/) for location)

### Step 2: Access the Coolant Valve

The coolant valve is located in the front of the vehicle, in the "penthouse" area above the front drive unit (or motor area on RWD).

1. Lift the front of the vehicle and secure on jack stands using proper [jack points](/posts/tesla-jack-points-lifting-guide/)
2. Remove the front aero shield/skid plate (multiple 10mm bolts and push clips)
3. Locate the coolant valve assembly â€” it has multiple coolant hoses and an electrical connector

### Step 3: Drain Coolant

1. Place the drain pan under the valve area
2. Carefully disconnect the lowest coolant hose from the valve â€” coolant will flow out
3. Allow approximately 2â€“3 liters to drain
4. You don't need to fully drain the system â€” just enough to work on the valve

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>Pro Tip:</strong> Open the coolant reservoir cap in the frunk to allow air in and speed up draining.
</div>

### Step 4: Remove the Old Valve

1. Disconnect the electrical connector from the coolant valve (press the release tab)
2. Use spring clamp pliers to release each coolant hose from the valve
3. Remove the mounting bolts securing the valve to the bracket (typically 10mm, **torque spec: 9 Nm / 7 ft-lbs** for reinstall)
4. Carefully wiggle the valve free from all hose connections
5. Have rags ready â€” residual coolant will spill

### Step 5: Install the New Valve

1. Compare the old and new valve â€” confirm identical port layout and connector
2. Apply a thin film of coolant to new O-rings for easier installation
3. Seat the new valve into position on the mounting bracket
4. Reconnect all coolant hoses and secure spring clamps
5. Torque mounting bolts to **9 Nm (7 ft-lbs)**
6. Reconnect the electrical connector until it clicks

### Step 6: Refill Coolant

1. Close any disconnected hoses
2. Fill the coolant reservoir with Zerex G48 50/50 to the MAX line
3. Reconnect the 12V battery
4. Turn on the vehicle and set HVAC to maximum heat
5. Monitor coolant level as the system purges air â€” top off as needed
6. Run the coolant valve self-test via touchscreen: **Service Mode > Thermal > Coolant System > Coolant Valve Test**

<div class="info-box">
<strong>Air Purge:</strong> The system may take 15â€“20 minutes of running to fully purge air. Keep topping off coolant as the level drops. You may hear gurgling sounds â€” this is normal.
</div>

### Step 7: Reinstall and Test

1. Reinstall the aero shield/skid plate
2. Lower the vehicle
3. Drive for 15â€“20 minutes and recheck coolant level
4. Verify no error codes have returned
5. Confirm cabin heating works properly
