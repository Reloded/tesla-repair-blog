## Option 1: Tesla Service Centers

**Best for:** Warranty repairs, complex parts, when you need Tesla to do the work anyway

Tesla's official service centers can order any part, but the experience varies:

**Pros:**
- Guaranteed genuine OEM parts
- Warranty coverage on repairs
- They handle everything

**Cons:**
- Expensive labor rates (â‚¬150-200/hour)
- Parts markup can be significant
- Appointments often 2-4 weeks out
- Some parts backordered for months
- Can't always buy parts without booking service

**When to use:** If your car is under warranty, or you need a complex part that requires programming (like a new MCU or drive unit), go through Tesla.

## Option 2: Tesla Online Shop

Tesla has an official parts shop at [shop.tesla.com](https://shop.tesla.com), though availability varies by country.

**Available parts typically include:**
- Cabin air filters
- Wiper blades
- Key fobs and key cards
- Wheel accessories
- Touch-up paint
- Basic accessories

**Pros:**
- Genuine Tesla parts
- No appointment needed
- Reasonable prices for accessories

**Cons:**
- Limited selection (mostly consumables)
- No mechanical parts
- Shipping can be slow
- Often out of stock

**When to use:** Good for routine maintenance items like [cabin air filters](/posts/tesla-cabin-air-filter/) or [wiper blades](/posts/tesla-windshield-wiper-replacement/) if you want genuine Tesla parts.

## Option 5: eBay and Online Marketplaces

eBay can be a source for used Tesla parts, but requires caution.

**Pros:**
- Sometimes the only source for rare parts
- Prices can be very low
- Buyer protection available

**Cons:**
- Quality varies enormously
- Shipping from US/China can take weeks
- Risk of damaged or incorrect parts
- Harder to return

**Tips for buying on eBay:**
- Check seller ratings and history
- Look for detailed photos
- Verify part numbers match your vehicle
- Factor in customs/VAT for non-EU sellers
- Avoid "too good to be true" prices

## OEM vs. Aftermarket vs. Used: What to Choose?

### Go OEM When:
- Safety-critical components (brakes, suspension bolts)
- Electrical/electronic parts
- Under warranty
- Part failure could strand you

### Aftermarket is Fine For:
- Cabin air filters
- Wiper blades
- Wheel accessories
- Interior trim pieces
- Touch-up paint

### Used Parts Work For:
- Body panels (check for damage)
- Interior trim
- Non-safety suspension components
- Cosmetic parts
- Expensive parts where OEM is overkill

## Part Numbers: How to Find Them

Before ordering, get the correct part number. Tesla uses specific part numbers that vary by model year and configuration.

**How to find your part number:**
1. **Tesla Service Manual** - If you have access
2. **Ask Tesla Service** - They can look it up
3. **Online parts catalogs** - Several sites list Tesla part numbers
4. **Salvage listings** - Often include part numbers

**Example part number format:** `1044691-00-B` (Model 3 front lower control arm)

Always verify the part number matches your specific vehicle before ordering.
