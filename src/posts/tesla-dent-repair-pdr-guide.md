## Understanding Tesla Body Panels

Before you attempt any dent repair, you need to know what your panels are made of. Tesla uses a mix of materials, and the repair approach differs significantly between them.

### Model 3 and Model Y

The Model 3 and Y use **steel** for most body panels (doors, hood, fenders, trunk lid, roof) with **aluminum** for the front and rear bumper reinforcement bars and some structural components. The bumper covers themselves are **plastic** (polypropylene).

Steel panels respond well to all dent repair methods — PDR, glue pulling, and traditional body filler.

### Model S and Model X

The Model S and X use **aluminum** extensively for body panels — doors, hood, trunk, fenders, and quarter panels. Aluminum is lighter but behaves differently than steel:

- **Work hardens** — aluminum becomes harder and more brittle each time it's deformed. A dent that's been pushed out and re-dented is much harder to fix the second time.
- **No magnetic tools** — magnetic dent pullers and holders won't stick to aluminum panels.
- **Requires specialized PDR techniques** — more finesse, slower pushing, and different tip selection.
- **Higher professional repair costs** — aluminum body work costs 1.5-2x more than steel.

### Tesla's Thin Paint

Tesla's paint is famously thin. Independent measurements consistently show total paint thickness of 90-120 microns across all models, compared to 120-150 microns for most other manufacturers. Some early Model 3s had areas under 80 microns.

For dent repair, this means:
- **PDR is actually easier** — thin, flexible paint is less likely to crack when the metal is pushed back
- **Body filler work is riskier** — less clear coat means less room for sanding and blending
- **Touch-up paint** may be needed even for PDR on larger dents if the paint cracked at the impact point

## Model 3 Highland and Model Y Juniper 2024+

The 2024 Model 3 Highland and 2024+ Model Y Juniper bring body panel and styling changes that affect PDR access and method selection.

### Model 3 Highland (2024+)

The Highland refresh retained the steel-dominant body structure of the previous Model 3 but introduced several changes relevant to dent repair:

- **New front bumper fascia** — restyled with integrated lower air curtains. PDR access behind the front bumper requires removing the bumper cover: 3 bolts along the lower edge plus 2 wheel arch liner screws per side. See our [front bumper removal guide](/posts/tesla-front-bumper-removal/).
- **Revised hood** — slightly longer, same gauge steel. PDR access unchanged from Gen 2 Model 3 (through wheel well with inner fender liner removed).
- **Rear quarter panels** — steel, same as Gen 2. PDR access via tail light pocket (remove tail light: 4x T20 Torx screws) or via rear trunk liner.
- **Ultra Red paint (2024+)** — one of Tesla's thicker paint applications at 110-130 microns. Stealth Grey and Pearl White run 90-110 microns. Ultra Red requires extra care during PDR to avoid heat-cracking at the impact point.

### Model Y Juniper (2024+)

The Juniper refresh changed the front and rear fascia significantly:

- **New front bumper** — integrated dual-layer lower grille with aerodynamic inserts. Full front bumper removal required for behind-panel PDR access (15 push clips plus 4 screws). See our [front bumper removal guide](/posts/tesla-front-bumper-removal/).
- **New rear bumper** — flush-mounted tail lights with horizontal LED strip. Accessing rear bumper dents from behind requires removing 3 push clips from the trunk floor liner to reach the upper access panel.
- **Door panels** — unchanged from pre-Juniper (steel). Standard PDR access via window channel or door card removal.
- **Acoustic glass (standard on Juniper)** — double-pane side windows are heavier. No change to door panel PDR access, but note the extra weight when removing door trim for rod insertion.

The Cybertruck uses 3 mm cold-rolled stainless steel exoskeleton panels. **PDR is not possible on stainless steel.** Glue pulling and body filler are equally ineffective. Any dent or crease on a Cybertruck panel requires full panel replacement. Tesla quotes €2,500-4,500 per exoskeleton panel (2026 pricing). Cybertruck owners need excellent comprehensive insurance coverage from day one.

## Method 1: Paintless Dent Removal (PDR) — The Best Option

PDR is the gold standard for dent repair. A skilled technician uses specialized metal rods to push the dent out from behind the panel, restoring the original shape without touching the paint. No fillers, no repainting, no color matching.

### When PDR Works

- ✅ Door dings and parking lot dents
- ✅ Hail damage (even heavy hail with 50+ dents)
- ✅ Dents where the paint is still intact (no cracks or chips)
- ✅ Dents with access behind the panel (most doors, hood, trunk, fenders)
- ✅ Round dents up to about 8-10 cm diameter

### When PDR Doesn't Work

- ❌ Paint is cracked, chipped, or flaking at the dent
- ❌ Sharp crease dents (metal stretched beyond recovery)
- ❌ Dents on panel edges or body lines where metal is doubled over
- ❌ Dents with no access behind the panel (some pillar and quarter panel areas)
- ❌ Previously repaired areas with body filler underneath

## Method 2: DIY Glue Pulling

Glue pulling is the most accessible DIY dent repair method. You attach a plastic tab to the dent with hot glue, then pull it out with a slide hammer or bridge puller. It's the same basic principle professionals use, just from the outside instead of pushing from behind.

### What You Need

- **Glue pulling kit** — includes plastic tabs in various sizes, a slide hammer or bridge puller, and a knockdown tool. Starter kits on [Amazon.com](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=dent+puller+kit+glue+tabs+slide+hammer&tag=diyrepair07-20) run $25-50. [Amazon.de](https://www.amazon.de/s?k=dent+puller+kit+glue+tabs+slide+hammer&tag=diyrepair-21): €30-55.
- **Hot glue gun** — adjustable temperature preferred. Use the lowest temperature that gives a strong bond
- **PDR glue sticks** — NOT regular craft glue. PDR glue is formulated for metal adhesion and clean release. [US](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=pdr+glue+sticks+black+high+strength&tag=diyrepair07-20) · [DE](https://www.amazon.de/s?k=pdr+glue+sticks+black+high+strength&tag=diyrepair-21)
- **Isopropyl alcohol** — for cleaning the panel before gluing. [US](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=isopropyl+alcohol+70+percent+automotive&tag=diyrepair07-20)
- **Heat gun or hair dryer** — for warming the panel in cold weather (glue won't stick to cold metal). [US](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=heat+gun+automotive+adjustable+temperature&tag=diyrepair07-20)

### Step-by-Step: DIY Glue Pulling

**1. Clean the dent area**

Wash the area thoroughly. Wipe with isopropyl alcohol to remove any wax, sealant, or oils. The glue needs to bond directly to the clear coat — any contamination weakens the pull.

**2. Choose the right tab**

Select a tab slightly smaller than the dent. If the dent is larger than your biggest tab, you'll do multiple pulls working from the edges inward. For round dents, use a round tab. For oblong dents, use an oval tab oriented along the dent's longest axis.

**3. Apply glue to the tab**

Heat the glue gun fully (2-3 minutes). Apply a generous amount of glue to the tab — enough to cover the entire contact surface with a slight dome. You want full contact, not just the edges.

**4. Place the tab**

Press the glued tab firmly onto the center of the dent. Hold it steady for 30-60 seconds, then let the glue cool completely (2-3 minutes in warm weather, 5+ minutes in cold). Don't rush this step — a weak bond means the tab pulls off before the dent comes out.

**5. Pull**

Attach the slide hammer to the tab. Pull with firm, controlled force — not a violent yank. You want to gradually draw the metal out. If the tab pops off before the dent moves, your glue bond wasn't strong enough (clean the area better, use more glue, or warm the panel).

For a bridge puller, position the feet on either side of the dent on undamaged metal, then turn the center bolt to lift the tab.

**6. Knock down high spots**

After pulling, you may have slight high spots (metal that came out too far). Use the knockdown tool (a nylon or Delrin punch) to gently tap these back down. Work carefully — it's easier to over-tap than to re-pull.

**7. Repeat**

Most dents require 3-10 pulls to fully remove. Each pull brings the metal a little closer to its original shape. Reposition the tab as needed to address different areas of the dent.

**8. Remove glue residue**

PDR glue releases cleanly from automotive clear coat. Peel off the remaining glue by hand or use isopropyl alcohol. If any glue residue remains, a plastic razor blade will lift it without scratching the clear coat.

### Tips for Better Results

- **Temperature matters.** Glue pulling works best at 15-25°C. Below 10°C, warm the panel with a heat gun first. Above 30°C, use a slower-setting glue or the bond may release too quickly.
- **Work from the outside in.** On larger dents, pull the edges first to reduce the overall size, then target the deepest point.
- **Don't over-pull.** An over-pulled area (high spot) is harder to fix than an under-pulled dent. Take your time.
- **Use a line board** — a [PDR reflection board](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=pdr+line+board+reflection&tag=diyrepair07-20) or a fluorescent light held at an angle reveals the dent's true depth. Looking straight at a dent is deceptive; the reflection shows the actual contour.
- **Practice first.** If you've never done this before, find a scrap hood or door at a junkyard and practice before working on your Tesla.

### Realistic Expectations

DIY glue pulling can produce excellent results on small, round door dings. For larger or more complex dents, expect to get the panel 70-80% of the way there. Getting that last 20% is where professional skill and tools (metal rods from behind) make the difference.

If you get the dent 80% out and the remaining imperfection bothers you, a PDR professional can finish the job for less than a full repair would have cost.

## Method 3: Boiling Water (Plastic Bumpers Only)

This method only works on **plastic bumper covers** — not metal body panels.

### When to Use

- Dent or deformation in the front or rear bumper cover
- The bumper has flexed inward but not cracked or torn
- Common after low-speed parking bumps

### How It Works

1. Boil a large pot of water
2. Pour the boiling water over the dented area of the bumper
3. Immediately reach behind the bumper and push the dent out while the plastic is soft
4. Pour cold water over the area to help the plastic retain its new shape

On Tesla, accessing the back of the front bumper may require removing the bumper cover (see our [front bumper removal guide](/posts/tesla-front-bumper-removal/)). The rear bumper is slightly easier to access from inside the trunk area.

### Limitations

- Only works on plastic bumper covers, never metal
- May not work on deep or sharp deformations
- Risk of burns — be extremely careful with boiling water
- Some bumper dents spring back within days as the plastic "remembers" its deformed shape

## Method 4: Traditional Body Repair (When PDR Isn't Possible)

When the paint is cracked, the metal is stretched, or the dent is too severe for PDR, traditional body repair is the remaining option.

### What's Involved

1. **Sand** the damaged area down to bare metal
2. **Apply body filler** (Bondo or similar) to fill the depression
3. **Shape and sand** the filler to match the panel contour
4. **Prime** the repair area
5. **Paint** with color-matched base coat
6. **Clear coat** and blend into surrounding paint

### Tesla-Specific Challenges

- **Color matching is hard.** Tesla's multi-coat colors (especially Midnight Silver Metallic and Ultra White with pearlescent layers) are notoriously difficult to match. Even Tesla's own body shops sometimes get it wrong.
- **Thin paint means less blending room.** With only 90-120 microns of paint to work with, sanding and blending requires a very light touch.
- **Aluminum panels need aluminum-specific filler.** Standard body filler on aluminum causes galvanic corrosion. Use filler specifically rated for aluminum (3M 01131 or equivalent).
- **Aluminum welding requires different equipment.** If structural repair is needed, aluminum requires TIG welding with argon gas shielding, not the MIG welding used for steel.

### Cost

Traditional body repair typically costs **€500-2,000+ per panel** at a Tesla-certified body shop. Independent shops may charge less but verify they have experience with Tesla's materials and paint systems.

For reference, see our [Tesla repair cost guide](/posts/tesla-repair-cost-guide/) for typical pricing across all repair types.

## Method 5: Suction Cup Dent Puller

The simplest and cheapest method — a large suction cup that you press onto the dent and pull. Available for under €10 at any auto parts store.

### When It Works

- Large, shallow dents on flat panels (hood, roof)
- The panel has flexed inward without sharp creases
- Think "someone sat on the hood" type dents

### When It Doesn't Work

- Small door dings (suction cup can't grip a small enough area)
- Sharp creases or lines
- Dents on curved surfaces (suction cup won't seal)
- Textured or damaged paint (suction cup won't hold)

This is worth trying as a first step because it costs almost nothing and takes 30 seconds. But manage expectations — it works on maybe 1 in 10 dents.

## Hail Damage: Full Recovery Guide

Hail is the most destructive dent scenario — a single storm can leave 50-200 dents across every horizontal surface. Here is exactly how to handle it.

### Step 1: Document Everything Within 24 Hours

Take photos and video of every affected surface under bright light. Use a [PDR reflection board](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=pdr+line+board+reflection&tag=diyrepair07-20) or a fluorescent strip light held at a low angle — this reveals dents that are invisible in direct sunlight. Document:

- Number of dents per panel (hood, roof, trunk, fenders, doors)
- Any dents with cracked or chipped paint (these need traditional repair, not PDR)
- Glass damage: windshield, sunroof, camera lenses

Submit your insurance claim within 24-48 hours. Hail is a comprehensive (non-fault) event — it does not raise premiums in most EU and US policies.

### Step 2: Assess Severity

| Damage Level | Description | Recommended Repair | Typical Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light (1-20 dents) | Coin-sized and smaller, paint intact | PDR specialist | 1-2 days |
| Moderate (20-80 dents) | Mix of sizes, mostly paint-intact | PDR specialist | 2-4 days |
| Heavy (80+ dents) | Large dents, some paint cracking | PDR plus partial respray | 1-2 weeks |
| Total loss | Major structural distortion or glass damage | Insurance total loss claim | N/A |

### Step 3: Find a Hail PDR Specialist

Hail PDR is a distinct specialty from single-dent PDR. Hail specialists have:

- **High-output LED lighting rigs** — overhead light bars for consistent illumination across large panels
- **Multi-session workflow** — hail removal typically takes 3-6 separate repair sessions as metal relaxes between rounds
- **Roof and hood experience** — most hail damage is on horizontal surfaces requiring headliner and inner fender removal for access

**EU hail season: May–August.** Book immediately after a storm — reputable hail PDR shops fill 4-6 weeks out in peak season.

### Glass Damage From Hail

- **Windshield** — Tesla OEM replacement: €850-1,200 at a Tesla Service Center (includes camera recalibration). Third-party glass shops: €500-800, but may require separate camera recalibration. See our [autopilot camera calibration guide](/posts/tesla-autopilot-camera-calibration/).

## Preventing Future Dents

Once your Tesla is dent-free, keep it that way:

- **Park strategically.** End spots, pull-through spots, and spots away from cart corrals reduce door ding risk dramatically.
- **Use paint protection film (PPF).** While PPF is primarily for paint chip protection, the thick urethane film also absorbs minor impacts that would otherwise dent the metal. See our [PPF installation guide](/posts/tesla-ppf-installation/).
- **Consider a vinyl wrap.** Adds a thin layer of impact protection on top of preventing stone chips. See our [vinyl wrap guide](/posts/tesla-vinyl-wrap-guide/).
- **Covered parking.** If you have access to a garage, use it. Eliminates hail risk entirely.
- **Door edge guards.** Cheap adhesive strips that protect the edges of your doors from hitting walls and other cars. Available in body-color options for a subtle look.
- **Ceramic coating won't help.** A common misconception — [ceramic coating](/posts/tesla-ceramic-coating/) protects against scratches and chemical damage, not physical dents.

## When to Use Insurance vs Pay Out of Pocket

### File a Claim When:

- Hail damage (dozens of dents) — comprehensive coverage, non-fault event
- Large dents requiring traditional body repair (€1,000+)
- Multiple panels are damaged
- Damage includes glass (windshield, sunroof, cameras)

### Pay Out of Pocket When:

- Single door ding — PDR cost (€75-150) is usually less than your deductible
- Small dents that a glue pull kit can handle (€30-60 for the kit)
- Your deductible is high (€500+)

Filing a comprehensive claim for hail damage does not raise your premiums in most EU markets since it is a non-fault event. Verify your specific Casco policy terms and deductible amount before deciding whether to claim.

## Summary: Which Method to Choose

| Method | Best For | DIY? | Cost | Result Quality |
|--------|----------|------|------|----------------|
| **Professional PDR** | Any dent with intact paint | No | €75-500 | Excellent (95-100%) |
| **DIY Glue Pull** | Small door dings | Yes | €30-60 (kit) | Good (70-90%) |
| **Boiling Water** | Plastic bumper dents only | Yes | Free | Variable (50-80%) |
| **Suction Cup** | Large shallow dents | Yes | €5-10 | Poor-Fair (20-50%) |
| **Traditional Body Repair** | Cracked paint, severe dents | No | €500-2,000+ | Excellent |
| **Panel Replacement** | Cybertruck exoskeleton, total loss | No | €2,500-4,500 | Factory |

**Our recommendation:** For most Tesla dents, start with a professional PDR quote. It is usually the best balance of cost, result quality, and preserving your Tesla's original paint. If the dent is a small door ding and you're handy, try DIY glue pulling first — worst case, you're out €30 and you take it to a PDR tech anyway.

Keep the paint intact whenever possible. Every Tesla panel that stays original paint is worth more at resale than one that has been resprayed, no matter how good the color match.
