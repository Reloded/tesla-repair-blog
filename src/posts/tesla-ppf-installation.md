## Surface Preparation

Preparation accounts for 70% of the result quality. Anything on the paint surface gets permanently sealed under the film.

### Step 1: Two-Bucket Wash (30 minutes)

1. Pre-rinse the entire car with low-pressure water to lift loose debris
2. Wash with pH-neutral car shampoo using a two-bucket method (wash + rinse bucket)
3. Pay extra attention to panel shut lines and under-lip areas where debris hides
4. Rinse completely and dry with a clean microfiber

### Step 2: Iron Decontamination (15 minutes)

1. Spray an iron fallout remover across the panel â€” liquid turns purple on contact with embedded iron particles
2. Rinse thoroughly
3. Inspect for remaining contamination â€” metallic specs visible against light

### Step 3: Clay Bar Treatment (20â€“30 minutes per panel)

1. Spray panel with clay lubricant
2. Work the clay bar in overlapping passes using light pressure
3. Panel should feel glass-smooth when done â€” rough = more contamination still present
4. Fold and re-knead clay frequently; discard if dropped

### Step 4: IPA Panel Wipe (5 minutes per panel)

1. Mix 50% IPA + 50% distilled water in a spray bottle
2. Spray and wipe panel with a clean microfiber â€” use a fresh microfiber fold for each pass
3. Do not touch the paint with bare hands after this step
4. Let dry completely (3â€“5 minutes) before film application

## Step-by-Step Application

### Slip and Tack Solutions

**Slip solution (positioning):**
- 1 liter distilled water
- 8 drops baby shampoo (no conditioner, no aloe)
- Mix gently â€” avoid foam

**Tack solution (final set):**
- 150ml distilled water
- 50ml IPA 99%
- Used in the final squeegee pass to help adhesive activate

### Step 1: Pre-Stage the Film (5 minutes)

1. Lay the pre-cut film piece on a clean, dry surface backing-side up
2. Confirm orientation against the panel before peeling
3. Spray the backing paper with slip solution (not the adhesive side yet)

### Step 2: Peel and Spray

1. Peel 5â€“10cm of backing paper from one edge
2. Spray the exposed adhesive with slip solution immediately
3. Continue peeling in sections of 10â€“15cm, spraying as you go
4. Never expose more than 30cm of adhesive at once outdoors

### Step 3: Position on Panel

1. Generously spray the paint surface with slip solution
2. Lay the film on the panel â€” it will float and remain repositionable for 30â€“60 seconds
3. Align using reference marks (edge of headlight, corner of bumper cap)
4. Work from largest reference point to smallest

### Step 4: Initial Squeegee Pass

1. Start at the center of the panel
2. Use a soft felt squeegee with gentle, overlapping strokes toward the edges
3. Apply consistent pressure â€” not heavy â€” to push solution out
4. Wipe excess solution off the surface as you go with a microfiber

### Step 5: Stretching Curves (Heat Gun Technique)

For curved areas (bumper corners, mirror caps):

1. Set heat gun to LOW (40â€“50Â°C air temperature â€” hold your hand 15cm away, should feel warm but not hot)
2. Keep the gun moving in sweeping passes â€” never stationary
3. Heat 10â€“15 seconds, then squeegee while film is warm and pliable

5. Surface temp goal: 45â€“55Â°C â€” use an IR thermometer if available

### Step 6: Edge Finishing

1. Squeegee firmly toward all edges â€” no solution trapped at the perimeter
2. For wrapped-edge installations: use a plastic trim tool to tuck the film under the bumper or hood edge (heat the film slightly first)
3. For overlap-edge installations: squeegee to within 2mm of the edge; do not try to fold it
4. Apply a final pass of tack solution and squeegee dry

### Step 7: Post-Install Inspection

- **Air bubbles:** Use a fine needle at a 45Â° angle; gently squeegee flat; if they return, the adhesive has released and the section must be reapplied

## Common DIY Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Cold Garage (<15Â°C)

Adhesive won't bond below 15Â°C surface temperature. The film appears to stick but fails within weeks as temperature cycles. Minimum: warm the garage to 18Â°C for 2 hours before installation.

### Mistake 2: Overheating (>70Â°C surface)

PPF adhesive begins to degrade above 70Â°C surface temperature. Excessive heat gun use leaves permanent stretch marks and can cause the protective top coat to dull. Set your heat gun to LOW and keep moving.

### Mistake 3: Insufficient Slip Solution

Under-lubricated film sticks on contact. You lose all repositioning ability. The solution: use more slip solution than feels necessary. The film should glide freely across the paint.

### Mistake 4: Working in Sections Too Large

On a Model Y hood (1.4m wide), applying the full piece at once is extremely difficult solo. Experienced installers work in 2-person teams on large panels. Solo: fold the film and work half at a time.

### Mistake 5: Skipping the IPA Wipe

Any wax, silicone, or detailing spray residue prevents adhesion. Always IPA-wipe even if the paint looks clean.

### Signs Your PPF Needs Replacing

- **Yellowing:** Visible amber tint on the clear film, most obvious on white or silver paint; accelerated by years of UV exposure. A good sign that the top coat has degraded.
