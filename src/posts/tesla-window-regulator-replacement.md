Is your Tesla window stuck, moving slowly, or making grinding noises? The window regulator is likely the culprit. This is one of the more common repairs on aging Teslas, especially 2017-2021 Model 3 and Model S vehicles.

Tesla service centers charge **$400-800** for this repair. With this guide, you can do it yourself for **$80-150 in parts** and about 2 hours of work.

## Symptoms of a Failing Window Regulator

Before ordering parts, confirm the regulator is actually the problem:

- **Window moves slowly or unevenly** â€” the motor struggles against worn regulator tracks
- **Grinding or clicking noise** when operating the window
- **Window falls into the door** â€” the cable or mechanism has snapped
- **Window won't go up** but you hear the motor running
- **Window tilts or binds** instead of moving straight up/down

If the window doesn't respond at all and you hear nothing, check the window switch and fuses firstâ€”that's a different problem.

## Step 1: Remove the Door Panel

The door panel removal is the same whether you're working on the front or rear windows.

1. **Lower the window partially** (if it still works) â€” this makes glass removal easier later

2. **Locate and remove the screws:**
   - Model 3/Y: Two T30 Torx screws behind small covers at the bottom of the panel
   - Model S: Three screwsâ€”two at bottom, one behind the door handle trim
   
3. **Pop off screw covers** using a plastic pry tool. They're delicateâ€”work carefully.

4. **Disconnect the door panel** starting from the bottom:
   - Insert your trim tool between the panel and door frame
   - Work around the edges, releasing plastic clips
   - Lift the panel upward to unhook it from the window sill

5. **Disconnect electrical connectors:**
   - Window switch connector
   - Door handle cable (push the clip and slide off)
   - Puddle lamp connector (if equipped)
   - Take photos before disconnectingâ€”you'll thank yourself later

6. **Set the panel aside** on a clean surface, face down on a towel to protect it.

## Step 2: Access the Window Regulator

With the door panel removed, you'll see a black plastic closeout panel covering the inner door cavity.

1. **Tape the window glass in the UP position:**
   - Use painter's tape across the door frame to hold the glass up
   - Apply tape on both sides of the glass for security
   - This prevents the glass from falling into the door when you disconnect it

2. **Remove the inner belt seal** â€” the rubber strip along the top of the door opening. It usually pulls off by hand.

3. **Remove the closeout panel:**
   - Carefully peel it away from the door frame
   - It's attached with adhesive and clips
   - Work slowly to avoid tearingâ€”you'll reuse this panel

4. **Disconnect the 12V battery** (recommended by Tesla):
   - This is especially important if your car has side airbags
   - Wait 1 minute after disconnecting before proceeding
   - Location: Under the hood for Model 3/Y, front trunk for Model S

## Step 3: Remove the Old Regulator

Now you can see the window regulator mechanism inside the door cavity.

1. **Locate the access holes** â€” there are plastic plugs covering the regulator mounting nuts. Pop these out with a small screwdriver.

2. **Disconnect the window motor electrical connector** â€” it's clipped to the regulator. Press the release tab and pull straight out.

3. **Support the window glass** â€” have a helper hold it from inside the door, or ensure your tape is secure.

4. **Remove the glass mounting hardware:**
   - Loosen the bolts that clamp the glass to the regulator
   - Carefully lift the glass out of the regulator track
   - Rest the glass safely inside the door or remove it completely

5. **Remove the regulator mounting nuts:**
   - There are 4 nuts (10mm) holding the regulator to the door
   - Remove all four nuts completely

6. **Remove the regulator:**
   - There's also a clip attaching it to the door structureâ€”pop this off
   - Maneuver the regulator out through the door opening
   - Tip: Remove one arm at a time, pivoting the assembly as you go
   - It takes some wigglingâ€”be patient

## Step 4: Install the New Regulator

Before installation, compare the old and new regulators side-by-side to confirm you have the correct part.

1. **Position the new regulator:**
   - Feed it into the door cavity one arm at a time
   - Align the mounting studs with the holes in the door

2. **Install mounting nuts (hand-tight only for now):**
   - Thread all 4 nuts onto the studs
   - Don't tighten yetâ€”you'll need adjustment room

3. **Reinstall the glass:**
   - Lower the glass into the regulator track carefully
   - Align the glass mounting bracket with the regulator clamps
   - Install the mounting bolts hand-tight

4. **Connect the motor electrical connector** â€” push until it clicks.

5. **Reconnect the 12V battery** so you can test and adjust.

## Step 5: Adjust and Final Torque

This is the critical step that determines whether your window seals properly.

1. **Loosen the lower regulator mounting nuts 3 turns** â€” this allows for angle adjustment

2. **Close the door** and raise the window fully using the switch

3. **Check alignment:**
   - Look at the gap between the glass and the door frame
   - The glass should be parallel to the frame, with even gaps all around
   - Check that it seals properly against the weather stripping

4. **Adjust if needed:**
   - Open the door
   - Turn the regulator mounting studs to change the glass angle
   - Use a 3mm Allen key on the jack screws to prevent them from spinning
   - Close door and recheckâ€”repeat until aligned

5. **Final torque all fasteners:**
   - Regulator mounting nuts: **10 Nm (7.4 lb-ft)**
   - Glass clamp bolts: **9.5 Nm (7.0 lb-ft)**

## Step 6: Reassembly and Calibration

1. **Install the access hole plugs**

2. **Reinstall the closeout panel** â€” press firmly around the edges to reseal

3. **Reinstall the inner belt seal**

4. **Reconnect the door panel connectors:**
   - Puddle lamp
   - Door handle cable (slide and click)
   - Window switch

5. **Reinstall the door panel:**
   - Hook the top edge onto the window sill first
   - Press around the edges to snap clips back into place
   - Install screws and covers

6. **Calibrate the window regulator:**

   **Method 1 â€” Touchscreen (2024+ software):**
   - Go to Controls > Service > Window Calibration
   - Follow the on-screen prompts
   
   **Method 2 â€” Manual calibration:**
   - Close the window fully
   - Hold the window UP button for 5 seconds after it stops
   - Open the window fully
   - Hold the window DOWN button for 5 seconds after it stops
   - Close the window again â€” it should now auto-close properly

## Troubleshooting

**Window still doesn't work:**
- Check the motor connector is fully seated
- Verify the 12V battery is reconnected
- Check for blown fuses (owner's manual has locations)

**Window moves but is crooked:**
- Loosen the glass clamp bolts and regulator nuts
- Adjust until the glass is parallel to the frame
- Re-torque and retest

**Auto-close doesn't work:**
- Repeat the calibration procedure
- Some cars require the touchscreen calibration method

**Grinding noise persists:**
- Check for debris in the window track
- Verify the glass is properly seated in the regulator guides
