The 2025+ Tesla Model Y Juniper is a major refresh — up to 50% of the parts are new compared to the pre-refresh Model Y. That means most old accessories **don't fit**. Different center console, reshaped doors, larger screen, new trunk design.

This guide covers every worthwhile accessory category with specific recommendations from real Juniper owners. We'll tell you what's actually worth buying, what's overpriced, and what pre-refresh parts to avoid.

## ⚠️ Compatibility Warning: Pre-Refresh Parts Don't Fit

Before you buy anything, check that it's specifically made for the **2025+ Model Y Juniper** (sometimes listed as "2026 Model Y" or "New Model Y"). Parts that changed and **need Juniper-specific versions**:

- Floor mats (different seat track layout)
- Center console organizers (completely redesigned console)
- Screen protectors (15.4" vs 15" screen)
- Door pocket organizers (new door card design)
- Trunk/cargo liners (reshaped trunk opening)
- Rear seat organizers (new 8" rear screen)

**Parts that still fit from pre-refresh:**
- Tire inflators and repair kits
- Phone mounts (most universal types)
- USB drives and SSDs for dashcam
- Wheel lug nut covers
- License plate frames
- Portable chargers (J1772/NACS)

## 1. All-Weather Floor Mats — Get These First

This is the single most important accessory. Tesla's carpet mats stain easily, and replacement carpet costs significantly more than a set of all-weather mats.

### Top Picks

**Premium: 3D Maxpider Kagu Mats**
- Full coverage including under-seat storage areas
- Textured surface that doesn't look like cheap rubber
- Raised edges catch water, mud, and snow
- Available in black or tan
- Around €100-130 for a full set

**Mid-Range: Tesmanian All-Weather Mats**
- Very popular in the Tesla community
- Good fit, no logos (looks factory)
- Covers front, rear, and cargo
- Around €80-100 for a full set

**Budget: Taptes or Generic TPE Mats**
- Clean design, no branding
- Surprisingly good fit for the price
- Available on Amazon
- Around €50-70 for a full set

> **Tip:** Whatever you buy, make sure the front mats cover the dead pedal area (left footrest). Cheap mats often skip this, leaving carpet exposed exactly where your left foot rests.

## 2. Screen Protector — Matte vs Glossy

The Juniper's 15.4-inch touchscreen is gorgeous but collects fingerprints and glares in direct sunlight. Some 2026 deliveries have come with factory-applied protectors, but many owners replace them.

### Matte (Anti-Glare) — Recommended
- Dramatically reduces sun glare while driving
- Hides fingerprints
- Slight texture feel when swiping
- Makes the screen look slightly less vibrant
- **Best for daily drivers, especially in sunny climates**

### Glossy (Clear)
- Maximum screen clarity and color
- Shows every fingerprint
- Glare can be an issue on sunny days
- Better for garage-parked cars

### Rear Screen Protector
The Juniper has an 8-inch rear passenger touchscreen. Protectors for this screen are also available — worth getting if you have kids who will be poking at it.

**Popular brands:** Spigen (well-known quality), Abstract Ocean, and various Amazon options. Look for "15.4 inch" specifically — 15-inch protectors from the old Model Y don't fit properly.

## 3. Center Console Organizer

The Juniper's center console is completely redesigned with a new sliding tray and deeper storage. A good organizer keeps small items from rattling around.

### What to Look For
- **Flocked (felt-lined) tray** — prevents items from sliding and reduces noise
- **Multiple compartments** — separate spots for cards, coins, sunglasses
- **USB pass-through** — some organizers include built-in USB-C and USB-A ports plus a 12V outlet
- **Armrest cover** — protects the lid from scratches and wear

**Spigen** makes a popular set with console organizer, under-screen cubby tray, and armrest cover as a bundle. RPM Tesla also offers a fully-flocked tray with integrated charging ports.

---

## 4. Mud Flaps — Protect Your Paint

Tesla doesn't include mud flaps from the factory, and the Juniper's lower ground clearance (0.2 inches less than pre-refresh) means more road debris hitting your paint.

- **Front and rear set** — essential in wet or gravel-prone areas
- **Rally Armor** — premium option, soft material that flexes
- **Generic Amazon sets** — much cheaper, slightly less refined fit
- Installation takes about 20 minutes with basic tools
- Some require removing wheel well liner clips

> **EU owners:** Mud flaps are especially important in winter with road salt and gravel. A €30 set can save you hundreds in paint correction later.

## 5. USB Hub & Dashcam Setup

The Juniper comes with three 65W USB-C ports (enough to charge a laptop!), but you'll want a dedicated dashcam setup.

### Recommended Setup
1. **Samsung T7 500GB SSD** — reliable, fast, perfect for Tesla dashcam and Sentry Mode
2. **USB-C Hub** — splits one port into multiple connections (dashcam SSD + phone charging)
3. **Format the SSD** as exFAT before first use

The Juniper's dashcam records from multiple cameras including the new **front bumper camera** — a first for Model Y. Make sure your SSD has enough space (500GB recommended for Sentry Mode).

## 6. Glass Roof Sunshade

The Juniper's glass roof has **7x better heat refraction** than the old Model Y thanks to a new silver coating. But in hot climates, a sunshade still makes a noticeable difference.

### Types
- **Mesh sunshade** — lets some light through, reduces heat significantly
- **Full blackout shade** — maximum heat and light reduction, makes cabin feel smaller
- **UV-blocking film** — permanent solution, professional installation recommended

Most Juniper owners in moderate climates find the improved factory glass sufficient. If you live somewhere with extreme heat (Southern Europe, Middle East, southern US), consider a mesh shade.

---

## 7. Trunk & Cargo Protection

The Juniper's trunk has a new squared-off rear glass shape and a quieter automatic closing mechanism. Protect the cargo area from scratches and spills.

### Must-Haves
- **Trunk cargo liner** — deep-sided, waterproof, covers full cargo area
- **Trunk sill protector** — clear film or hard protector on the loading edge (prevents scratches from loading/unloading)
- **Rear seat back cover** — protects the back of folded seats from cargo scratches
- **Grocery bag hooks** — cheap and surprisingly useful

**Jowua** makes a popular trunk liner + rear seat back cover bundle for the Juniper. Weathertech and Tesmanian also have Juniper-specific options.

---

## 8. Phone Mount

Tesla's built-in wireless charging pad works for most phones, but a mounted phone is better for navigation apps like Waze or for passengers who want their own screen.

### Options
- **MagSafe mount** — magnetic, clean look, easy on/off (requires MagSafe-compatible phone or case)
- **Vent clip mount** — attaches to the AC vent, no adhesive
- **Screen-side mount** — attaches near the touchscreen, keeps phone in your line of sight
- **Jowua Active Cooling Mount** — prevents phone overheating during wireless charging (common Tesla issue)

> **Tip:** Avoid suction cup mounts on the glass roof — they tend to fall off in heat and can damage the coating.

## 9. Wheel & Tire Accessories

### Aero Wheel Covers
The Juniper's Gemini wheels have a new design. Aftermarket aero covers can improve range by 3-5% and give a different look.

### Lug Nut Covers
Tesla lug nuts are exposed and prone to rust. A set of lug nut covers costs under €15 and keeps them looking clean. These are universal and fit both pre-refresh and Juniper.

### Tire Inflator
Keep a portable tire inflator in the trunk. Tesla doesn't include a spare tire, so a quick inflation and sealant kit can get you to the nearest service point.

---

## 10. Interior Lighting & Ambient Upgrades

The Juniper already comes with **multicolor RGB ambient lighting** extending from the dash through all four doors — a significant upgrade over the old Model Y.

For owners wanting more:
- **Footwell LED strips** — additional lighting under the dash
- **Trunk light upgrade** — brighter LED for cargo area visibility
- **Frunk light** — the Juniper's frunk now has a drain hole (great for using as a cooler) but limited lighting

> **Note:** Unlike the old Model Y, the Juniper's built-in ambient lighting is customizable via the touchscreen. Many owners find aftermarket LED kits unnecessary with the Juniper.

## 11. Paint Protection

### Paint Protection Film (PPF)
The Juniper's new front fascia is especially prone to rock chips. Key areas to protect:
- Front bumper and hood leading edge
- Side mirror caps
- Rocker panels
- Door handle areas
- Trunk sill/loading edge

### Ceramic Coating
A ceramic coating on top of the paint makes cleaning easier and adds a layer of protection. Professional application runs €500-1,500 depending on coverage. DIY ceramic sprays are available for €30-50 but don't last as long.

## What's NOT Worth Buying

Not every Tesla accessory is worth your money. Skip these:

- **Performance pedal covers** — look cool for a week, then you forget about them
- **Fake carbon fiber trim overlays** — cheap-looking on the Juniper's improved interior
- **Rear spoiler** — the Juniper's improved aerodynamics (0.22 Cd) are already optimized
- **Door handle protectors** — the Juniper's flush handles rarely get scratched
- **Cheap wireless phone charger replacements** — the Juniper's built-in pad is already powerful with 15W charging
