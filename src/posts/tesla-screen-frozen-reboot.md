**Every Tesla owner hits this at some point** — the touchscreen freezes, goes black, or stops responding to taps. The fix is almost always a quick reboot that takes 30 seconds.

> **TL;DR:** Hold both scroll wheels on the steering wheel for 10 seconds. Screen goes black, then restarts. Done.

Your car is completely safe during a reboot. It only restarts the infotainment system — driving, braking, and Autopilot all keep working.

## Soft Reboot (Fixes 95% of Issues)

This is the standard fix for a frozen or unresponsive screen. Do this first.

### How to Soft Reboot

1. **Shift into Park**
2. **Press and hold both scroll wheels** on the steering wheel simultaneously
3. **Hold until the screen turns black**
4. **Release the scroll wheels**
5. **Wait approximately 30 seconds** for the touchscreen to restart

This is Tesla's [official procedure](https://service.tesla.com/docs/Public/diy/modely/en_us/GUID-C6EE3503-1D68-455C-8AA3-2D6FF9ADCF5E.html). Note: the scroll wheel layout differs slightly on vehicles manufactured before vs after February 2025, but the procedure is the same.

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>Tesla's official warning:</strong> Only restart the touchscreen while the vehicle is stopped and in Park. The car status display, safety warnings, backup camera, etc. will not be visible during the restart.
</div>

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>If it freezes while driving:</strong> The car drives fine without the screen. Pull over safely and shift into Park before rebooting. You'll temporarily lose the speedometer display (Model 3/Y), navigation, and music — but driving functions remain active.
</div>

### What a Soft Reboot Resets

- ✅ Touchscreen and infotainment
- ✅ Bluetooth connections
- ✅ Media playback
- ✅ Navigation
- ❌ Does NOT reset driving systems
- ❌ Does NOT reset Autopilot
- ❌ Does NOT affect the car's ability to drive

## Hard Reboot (When Soft Reboot Doesn't Work)

If the soft reboot doesn't fix the problem — or the screen stays completely black — try a hard reboot. This method is widely used in the Tesla community, though it's not in Tesla's official DIY docs.

### How to Hard Reboot

1. **Shift into Park**
2. **Press and hold the brake pedal**
3. **While holding the brake, press and hold both scroll wheels**
4. **Hold everything for 10+ seconds** until the screen goes black
5. **Release all three** (brake + both scroll wheels)
6. **Wait 1-2 minutes** for a full restart

The hard reboot resets the infotainment computer more thoroughly than the soft version. If this still doesn't work, try [power cycling the vehicle](#full-power-cycle-nuclear-option) instead — that's Tesla's official next step.

### When to Use a Hard Reboot

- Soft reboot didn't fix the issue
- Screen is completely black (not just frozen)
- Severe glitches after a software update
- Multiple systems acting weird simultaneously (audio, Bluetooth, navigation all broken)

## Full Power Cycle (Nuclear Option)

If both reboots fail, you can fully power down the car:

1. **Shift into Park**
2. On the touchscreen, touch **Controls > Safety > Power Off**
3. **Wait at least 2 minutes** without interacting with the vehicle — do not open the doors, touch the brake pedal, touch the touchscreen, etc.
4. After 2 minutes, **press the brake pedal or open the door** to wake the vehicle

This is Tesla's [official power cycling procedure](https://service.tesla.com/docs/Public/diy/modely/en_us/GUID-76C3CAB5-8F3F-4F0A-93F4-966DB3E67FEA.html).

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>Important:</strong> You must wait the full 2 minutes without any interaction. If you touch anything early, the car wakes up before fully powering down and the cycle is incomplete.
</div>

If the screen is too frozen to access Controls, you can force a power-down by disconnecting the 12V battery — but that's a more involved process. See our [12V battery guide](/posts/tesla-12v-battery-replacement/) for details.

## Common Reasons Your Tesla Screen Freezes

### 1. Software Bugs

The most common cause. Tesla pushes frequent over-the-air updates, and sometimes a version has a bug that causes freezing. Usually fixed in the next update.

**What to do:** Reboot and wait for the next software update. Check online forums to see if others are reporting the same issue on your software version.

### 2. Heavy App Usage

Running Spotify, navigation, and the web browser simultaneously can overwhelm the infotainment system, especially on older hardware (MCU1).

**What to do:** Close extra apps. Avoid using the web browser while streaming music and navigating.

### 3. Low 12V Battery

A weak 12V battery doesn't just prevent your car from starting — it can cause random screen freezes, reboots, and glitches while driving.

**What to do:** If you're getting frequent random freezes, [check your 12V battery health](/posts/tesla-12v-battery-replacement/). Tesla Service can test it, or you can check the voltage yourself (should be 12.4V+ when parked).

### 4. Overheating

In extreme heat, the touchscreen can become sluggish or freeze. The screen has thermal protection that dims or disables it above certain temperatures.

**What to do:** Park in shade, use cabin overheat protection, and let the car cool down. The screen recovers on its own once temperatures drop.

### 5. Aging MCU (Media Control Unit)

The MCU is the computer behind the touchscreen. In older Teslas (2012-2018 Model S/X with MCU1), the built-in flash memory degrades over time, causing progressively worse freezing and slowdowns.

**What to do:** If you have an older Tesla with MCU1 and freezes are getting more frequent, you may need an MCU upgrade. Tesla offers an MCU2 retrofit for ~$1,500-2,000.

## MCU1 vs MCU2 vs MCU3: Which Do You Have?

| Feature | MCU1 | MCU2 | MCU3 (HW4) |
|---------|------|------|-------------|
| **Found in** | 2012-2018 Model S/X | 2018+ Model S/X, all Model 3/Y pre-2024 | 2024+ all models |
| **Processor** | Tegra 3 | Intel Atom | AMD Ryzen |
| **Common issues** | Frequent freezes, eMMC failure, slow | Occasional freezes | Rare issues |
| **Reboot time** | 60-90 seconds | 30-45 seconds | 15-25 seconds |
| **Upgradeable** | Yes → MCU2 | No | No |

### How to Check Your MCU Version

1. Tap the **Tesla "T" logo** at the top of the screen
2. Look at **Additional Vehicle Information**
3. Check the infotainment processor listed

## When a Frozen Screen Means a Real Problem

Rebooting fixes most screen issues. But if you're experiencing any of these, it's time for Tesla Service:

### Schedule an Appointment If:

- **Screen freezes multiple times per week** and rebooting is only a temporary fix
- **Screen goes black randomly** while driving (not just after parking)
- **Yellow border appears** around the screen edge (eMMC failure sign on MCU1)
- **Screen takes 5+ minutes to boot** after a reboot
- **Touchscreen is unresponsive in specific areas** (dead zones — hardware failure)
- **Screen flickers or shows artifacts** (display panel or ribbon cable issue)

### eMMC Failure (MCU1 Specific)

Older Model S/X vehicles with MCU1 are prone to eMMC flash memory failure. Symptoms:
- Progressively slower screen
- Screen freezes become more frequent over months
- Eventually the screen won't boot at all
- Yellow border around the touchscreen

Tesla extended the warranty on MCU1 eMMC to 8 years/100,000 miles due to a recall. If you're within that window, the replacement is free.

## Screen Care Tips

Prevent issues before they happen:

- **Install a screen protector** — prevents micro-scratches that worsen glare and protects against accidental damage
- **Don't use household glass cleaners** — they contain ammonia that damages the oleophobic coating
- **Use a microfiber cloth** with water or screen-specific cleaner
- **Avoid pressing hard** — the touchscreen is glass, but the digitizer underneath is sensitive
- **Keep software updated** — most screen bugs get patched quickly
