## Pre-Installation Preparation

### Step 1: Plan Your Layout

Before touching any trim panels, decide which zones you want to light up. Most owners install:

1. **Dashboard** â€” the signature strip across the dash (highest impact)
2. **Front doors** â€” along the door trim or speaker grille area
3. **Footwells** â€” subtle underglow for front and rear passengers
4. **Center console** â€” around the cup holder area or wireless charger
5. **Rear doors** â€” matches the front for a complete look
6. **Seat-backs** â€” accent halos visible to rear passengers

You don't have to install everything at once. Many kits are modularâ€”start with the dashboard and doors, then add footwells and rear zones later.

### Step 2: Test Everything Before Installing

This is the single most important tip in this entire guide. Before removing a single trim panel:

1. Lay out all components on a clean surface
2. Connect the control module to a USB power source or 12V supply
3. Plug in each light strip and verify it works
4. Test the app connection (download the kit's app beforehand)
5. Check every color mode and brightness level

If something is DOA, it's far easier to get a replacement before your car is in pieces.

### Step 3: Clean All Mounting Surfaces

Once you've verified everything works:

- Use isopropyl alcohol wipes to clean every surface where adhesive strips will mount
- Apply 3M 94 adhesive promoter on textured plastic surfaces for stronger bonding
- Let surfaces dry completely (2â€“3 minutes) before applying any strips

## Step-by-Step Installation

### Step 4: Remove Trim Panels

This is the most intimidating part, but Tesla's interior panels are designed to be removed with plastic pry tools. Take your timeâ€”rushing leads to broken clips.

#### Lower Dash Panels (Driver and Passenger Side)

1. Locate the lower panel beneath the dashboard on each side
2. Use a plastic pry tool to gently release the clips along the bottom edge
3. Work from one side to the otherâ€”don't yank the whole panel at once
4. Disconnect any connectors (the driver side may have a connector for the OBD-II port area)
5. Set panels aside on a soft surface

#### Center Console Side Panels

1. Remove the cup holder rubber insert
2. On kits with a replacement cup holder panel, pry out the existing cup holder surround
3. Release the clips on the side panels of the center tunnel (one on each side)
4. These panels run from the cup holder area down toward the rear seats

#### Door Panels (Front)

1. Use a T20 Torx bit to remove the bolt hidden behind the door handle area (covered by a small plastic capâ€”pry it off gently)
2. Starting from the bottom, use pry tools to release the clips around the perimeter
3. Lift the panel up and off the door frame
4. **Carefully disconnect the window switch connector and any speaker wiring** before fully removing the panel
5. If your kit includes replacement door trim strips, separate the factory trim piece from the door panel now

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>Important:</strong> Door panels have multiple wiring connectors. Take photos before disconnecting anything so you know exactly where each plug goes during reassembly. Label connectors with masking tape if you're removing multiple doors at once.
</div>

#### Door Panels (Rear)

1. Similar process to the front but simplerâ€”fewer connectors
2. Remove the T20 bolt behind the handle cover
3. Pry clips around the perimeter starting from the bottom
4. Disconnect the window switch connector

#### Upper Dashboard Trim (Advanced Kits Only)

Some premium kits (like Plugged In Performance) require removing the entire upper dashboard trim piece to replace it with a laser-etched version. This is the most complex step:

1. Remove the lower dash panels first (already done)
2. Carefully pry the upper trim starting from the edges
3. Work slowlyâ€”the dash trim has many clips and runs the full width of the car
4. **Do not force it**â€”if a section won't release, look for a hidden screw or clip you missed

Most standard kits use adhesive-mount strips that slide *behind* the existing trim, so you may not need this step at all.

### Step 5: Route the Wiring

With panels removed, you can see the routing paths for your wiring harnesses:

1. **Start at the control module location** â€” typically under the center console or in the cup holder area
2. Route the dashboard strip cable up through the gap behind the lower dash panel and along the back of the dashboard
3. Route door cables along the door sill â€” tuck wires under the existing sill trim pieces
4. For rear footwells, run cables under the center console side panels toward the rear seats
5. Use zip ties every 15â€“20 cm to secure cables to existing wire looms or mounting points
6. **Never route wires near airbag modules, seat belt pretensioners, or moving parts**

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>Warning:</strong> Keep all wiring away from airbag deployment zones. Running a wire behind an airbag module can interfere with deployment or cause injury in a crash. If you're unsure where airbags are located, check your owner's manual or the Tesla service manual.
</div>

### Step 6: Mount the LED Strips

1. **Dashboard strip**: Slide or adhesive-mount the strip into the groove between the upper and lower dash sections. Most kits are designed to friction-fit into this gap. The light faces downward, creating a soft glow across the dash surface.

2. **Door strips**: Mount along the door trim line, typically where the factory trim piece meets the door card. On kits with replacement trim pieces, the LEDs are pre-installed in the new trimâ€”just swap and connect.

3. **Footwell lights**: Mount under the dash (front) or under the front seats (rear). Adhesive-mount or clip into existing mounting points. Aim the light downward toward the floor.

4. **Center console**: Mount the strip around the cup holder area or along the sides of the center tunnel. Some kits include a replacement cup holder surround with integrated lighting and touch controls.

### Step 7: Connect the Power

Most aftermarket ambient lighting kits offer two power options:

#### Option A: USB Power (Simplest)

- Plug the control module's USB cable into the center console USB port or glovebox USB port
- **Pro**: No wiring, fully reversible, no warranty concerns
- **Con**: Uses a USB port, light stays on briefly after car sleeps (unless you get an auto-shutoff dongle)

#### Option B: 12V Hardwire (Recommended for Clean Install)

- Connect to the car's 12V circuit so lights turn on/off with the car automatically
- Most kits include a fuse tap or piggyback connector
- Common tap points:
  - **Fuse box under the dash** (driver side) â€” tap into an accessory circuit
  - **12V outlet behind the center console** (if equipped)
  - **OBD-II port power pins** â€” some kits include an OBD-II power adapter

For 12V hardwiring:
1. Locate the fuse box under the driver-side dash panel (already removed)
2. Identify an appropriate accessory fuse slot (one that's powered only when the car is "on")
3. Insert the fuse tap, connecting the kit's positive wire
4. Ground the negative wire to a chassis ground bolt nearby
5. Test that lights turn on when you open the door/wake the car, and off when the car sleeps

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>Don't tap into critical fuses.</strong> Avoid circuits for airbags, ABS, steering, or the main computer. Stick to low-current accessory circuits like the 12V outlet or interior lighting circuit.
</div>

### Step 8: Install the Control Module

1. Place the control module in its designated spot (usually under the center console or in the cup holder area)
2. Connect all strip harnesses to the control module â€” most use labeled plug connectors
3. Secure the module with adhesive or the included mounting bracket
4. Ensure the Bluetooth antenna (if separate) is positioned where it won't be blocked by metal panels

### Step 9: Reassemble Trim Panels

Now reverse the disassembly process:

1. Reconnect all door panel wiring connectors (check your photos!)
2. Align door panels and press firmly on each clip location until you hear/feel them snap in
3. Reinstall T20 bolts and handle covers
4. Snap center console side panels back into place
5. Reinstall lower dash panels
6. Replace cup holder insert (or install the kit's replacement panel)

**Take your time here.** Misaligned clips lead to rattles and buzzes that will drive you crazy at highway speeds.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Not testing before installing**
We said it before, but it's worth repeating. Test every single strip and the control module before removing any panels. Returning a defective kit after installation is a nightmare.

**2. Using too much force on trim clips**
Tesla's plastic clips are strong but not indestructible. If a panel won't release, look for a hidden fastener rather than pulling harder. Replacement clips are cheap but sourcing them takes time.

**3. Poor adhesive bonding**
The 3M adhesive on most strips works wellâ€”but only on clean, dry surfaces. Skip the alcohol wipe step and your strips will fall off within weeks, especially in hot climates. Use 3M 94 promoter on textured plastic.

**4. Routing wires across moving parts**
Don't route cables through door hinges without proper protection. Use the existing rubber wire boot/grommet that Tesla's own wiring passes through between the door and body.

**5. Choosing overly bright settings**
Ambient lighting should be subtle. If it's brighter than your screen at night, it's too bright. Start at 20â€“30% brightness and work up. Your eyes will thank you on long night drives.

**6. Ignoring the auto-shutoff problem**
USB-powered kits can drain your 12V battery if the lights don't turn off when the car sleeps. Either hardwire to an accessory circuit or buy an auto-shutoff dongle (~â‚¬10) that cuts power 30â€“60 seconds after the car locks.

**7. Forgetting to reconnect door panel wiring**
After reassembly, test every window switch, door lock button, and speaker before calling the job done. A forgotten connector means removing the panel again.

## Warranty Considerations

Adding aftermarket ambient lighting does **not** automatically void your Tesla warranty. Under the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act (in the US) and similar EU consumer protection laws, a manufacturer must prove that your modification caused the specific failure they're denying warranty coverage for.

That said, be smart about it:

- **Plug-and-play USB kits** carry virtually zero warranty risk
- **Fuse tap installations** are low risk if done correctly on an appropriate circuit
- **Splicing into factory wiring** is higher risk and generally not recommended
- If Tesla service needs to remove panels for a repair, they may not reinstall your aftermarket lighting
- Keep your installation reversible whenever possible

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does installation take?**
A footwell-only kit takes 20â€“30 minutes. A full-cabin kit with dashboard, doors, footwells, and center console takes 2â€“4 hours depending on your experience level and the specific kit.

**Will ambient lights drain my Tesla's battery?**
Modern LED ambient kits draw 3â€“6 watts, which is negligible when the car is running. The concern is when parkedâ€”USB-powered kits may stay on for a while after the car sleeps. Hardwired kits on an accessory circuit turn off automatically. Either way, the draw is too small to meaningfully affect your driving range.

**Can I install ambient lighting myself with no experience?**
Yes, if you choose a plug-and-play kit and follow the manufacturer's video guide. Trim panel removal is the hardest part, but it's more about patience than skill. If you can assemble IKEA furniture, you can install ambient lights.

**Do I need to disconnect the 12V battery?**
For USB-powered kits, no. For 12V hardwire installations, it's a good safety practice to disconnect the 12V battery first, though many installers skip this step for simple fuse taps. If you do disconnect it, note that your car may need to recalibrate some settings afterward.

**What's the best color for a factory look?**
Cool white or ice blue most closely matches Tesla's existing interior lighting. Most owners start here and then experiment with other colors for fun.

**Can I keep my ambient lights if I sell the car?**
Plug-and-play kits can be easily removed. Hardwired kits with replacement trim pieces are harder to reverse. If you plan to sell, consider keeping the original trim pieces so you can swap them back.

**My kit's app is in Chineseâ€”is that normal?**
Unfortunately, yes, for many AliExpress-sourced kits. Most apps have an English language option buried in settings. The "Happy Lighting" app (used by many generic kits) supports English. Some premium brands like SATONIC and Yeslak have fully English apps.

**Will this work on a 2024+ Model 3 Highland or 2025+ Model Y Juniper?**
These models come with factory ambient lighting. You can still install aftermarket kits for more coverage or color options, but make sure you buy a kit specifically designed for Highland/Juniperâ€”the interior trim, USB ports, and wiring are different from pre-refresh models.
