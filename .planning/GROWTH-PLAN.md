# Growth Plan — Tesla DIY Repair Blog (2026-06-15)

## The honest situation
- Revenue ≈ $0. **This is a traffic problem, not a setup problem.**
- Jan data: 2 Google clicks, 167 impressions, avg position 13.6 (page 2). ~500 pageviews/mo.
- To hit $1,000/mo on Amazon affiliate (~$1–2/sale, ~1% buy) you need **~50–100k visits/mo** — roughly 100x today.
- Pruning to 41 pages (done 2026-06-15) removes a likely *penalty*. It clears the runway; it does not add traffic by itself.
- Recovery from a content cleanup takes **weeks to months** in Google. Watch GSC for a slow inflection, not an overnight jump.

## Your unfair advantages (lean into these — AI farms can't copy them)
1. **You are a real, working Tesla mechanic** (RR Car Parts, Lithuania). Real hands, real tools, real repairs.
2. **Real workshop photos** of actual jobs.
3. **EU / Lithuania angle** — most Tesla content is US-centric. Local + Lithuanian-language = near-zero competition.
4. **An employer that sells genuine OEM Tesla parts** — a real, higher-margin monetization path than Amazon pennies.

---

## Pillar 1 — E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)
Google rewards demonstrable real-world expertise. Make yours unmissable.
- [ ] **Real author identity:** full name, photo, one-line credential ("Tesla technician at RR Car Parts, [N] years, [N]+ repairs"). Put a byline on *every* article linking to /about.
- [ ] **Strengthen /about:** your story, the workshop, tools you own, models you've worked on, why you started this. Add LinkedIn + any public profile. (Person schema already present — keep it accurate.)
- [ ] **Proof on each guide:** "Verified against Tesla service manual" line + date; "Photos from our workshop" where true.
- [ ] **First-person, specific detail** in guides: torque you actually used, the bolt that always seizes, the trick that saved an hour. This is the moat.
- [ ] **Contact + real business presence** (RR Car Parts address/relationship) — trust signal for both Google and buyers.

## Pillar 2 — Backlinks & authority (the #1 missing ingredient, budget-friendly)
No link buying. Earn links by being a real expert.
- [ ] **Journalist requests** (Featured.com / Qwoted / HARO-style): answer Tesla/EV repair queries → quoted with a link. ~30 min/week, free.
- [ ] **Communities as a person, not a marketer:** TMC (Tesla Motors Club), r/TeslaSupport, r/TeslaLounge, EV forums. Genuinely helpful answers; link only when it truly helps. (See past Reddit lessons in CLAUDE.md — casual tone, no listicles, verify specs.)
- [ ] **Guest posts** on EV blogs and Lithuanian/Baltic auto sites.
- [ ] **Local citations:** link from RR Car Parts site, Google Business, Lithuanian business/auto directories.
- [ ] **Linkable asset:** publish one original data piece only you can make — e.g., real repair-cost data from the shop, or a "most common Tesla failures we see in the Baltics" report. People link to original data.
- [ ] **Supplier features:** ask affiliate partners (Lectron, iFixit) to feature/link your guides.

## Pillar 3 — Win where you can actually rank (keyword strategy)
- [ ] **Stop chasing head terms.** Target long-tail, low-difficulty: specific error/alert codes, model-year-specific issues, "in Europe / EU" variants.
- [ ] **Lithuanian-language guides** for your best topics — likely #1 with little competition, and they funnel local leads to RR Car Parts.
- [ ] Use GSC "Queries" to find terms where you're position 8–20 ("striking distance") and improve those exact pages first.

## Pillar 4 — Don't depend on Google alone
- [ ] **YouTube repair videos** of real jobs (you already do the work). Huge repair-intent audience, strong E-E-A-T signal, embeddable on the matching blog post. Highest-leverage new channel.
- [ ] **Lithuanian/Baltic EV Facebook groups** — big locally; share genuinely useful guides.
- [ ] Email list (Buttondown already set up, 1 subscriber): add a real reason to subscribe; send when you have something worth sending.

## Pillar 5 — Rethink monetization (stop optimizing pennies)
- [ ] **Make RR Car Parts the hero** on high-intent repair pages (OEM parts, real margin) instead of leading with Amazon.
- [ ] **Local lead-gen:** "Need this done? Get a quote / book the repair" CTA on EU/Lithuania traffic — converts far better than affiliate clicks.
- [ ] **Hold on display ads** (Ezoic/AdSense) until traffic justifies it; they pay ~nothing at current volume and slow the site.

## 90-day sequence (you have evenings/weekends — keep it small and consistent)
**Weeks 1–2 — foundation**
- Confirm cron stopped; merge the prune. Submit updated sitemap in GSC.
- Add author byline + bio across guides; strengthen /about.
- Set up Featured/Qwoted; write 2 genuinely helpful forum answers.

**Weeks 3–6 — depth + first links**
- Improve your 5 "striking distance" pages (GSC position 8–20) with real first-hand detail.
- Publish 1 Lithuanian-language version of your best guide.
- Film + publish 1 YouTube repair video; embed on the matching post.
- 2 forum answers/week; pitch 1 guest post.

**Weeks 7–12 — compounding**
- Publish the original "repair data" linkable asset; promote it.
- 1 more LT-language guide; 1–2 more YouTube videos.
- Add RR Car Parts hero CTA + local lead-gen CTA to top EU pages.
- Review GSC: are impressions/positions trending up post-prune?

## What to measure (monthly, via `npm run gsc` / `npm run analytics`)
- Impressions & avg position trend (should improve first, before clicks).
- Number of referring domains (backlinks) — the leading indicator of authority.
- Clicks from search; assisted conversions to RR Car Parts / leads.
- Don't judge on revenue for the first ~3 months — judge on impressions, positions, and links.

## What NOT to do
- ❌ Restart mass AI content generation (that's what got the domain suppressed).
- ❌ Buy backlinks / use PBNs.
- ❌ Chase high-competition head terms with a low-authority domain.
- ❌ Expect overnight results — this is a 3–6 month rebuild.
