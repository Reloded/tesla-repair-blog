# Backlink Kit — Pillar 2 (2026-07-03)

Backlinks are the #1 thing standing between your page-2 rankings and page 1. No link buying, no PBNs — you earn links by being a real, findable Tesla mechanic. Everything here leans on your unfair advantages: you actually fix Teslas, you have a real workshop (RR Car Parts, Kaunas), and you have the EU/Baltic angle almost nobody else covers.

> **Voice rule (from hard experience — see CLAUDE.md Reddit notes):** everything you post publicly must sound like a busy mechanic typing on his phone, not a blog. Casual, short, specific, no numbered lists, no marketing tone. You were called out as AI before — the fix is real detail only you would know ("the T27 on the left caliper always seizes on salt-belt cars"). When in doubt, shorter. Verify any spec/year before you state it.

---

## 1. Journalist requests (highest ROI, ~30 min/week, free)
Sign up: **Featured.com**, **Qwoted**, **SourceBottle** (EU-friendly). Reporters post questions; you answer; if used, you get a named quote + a link to your site. Filter for "Tesla", "EV", "car repair", "EV ownership", "winter EV".

**Template — adapt every time, never paste verbatim:**
> I'm an independent Tesla mechanic in Lithuania — I work on Model 3/Y/S/X daily at an OEM parts workshop, so I see what actually fails in the field (especially in cold Baltic climates).
>
> [2–4 sentences answering their exact question, with one concrete, specific detail from real work — a part that fails often, a real cost range you've seen, a mistake owners make. No fluff.]
>
> Happy to expand or send a photo from the workshop. More on this: [link to the single most relevant guide].

**Rules:** answer within a few hours (early = used). One specific number or part beats three generic sentences. Only link when the guide genuinely covers their question. Give a real name for the byline.

## 2. Forums & communities (referral traffic + slow authority)
Targets: **Tesla Motors Club (TMC)**, **r/TeslaSupport**, **r/TeslaModel3/Y**, Lithuanian/Baltic EV Facebook groups. Most forum links are "nofollow" — you're doing this for *referral traffic + brand*, and because genuinely helpful answers occasionally get you a followed link or a mention elsewhere.

**Do:** answer a specific repair question from real experience, casually. Drop a guide link only if it directly answers what they asked and adds detail you can't fit in a comment. Build a few helpful comments before ever linking.

**Don't:** post the same phrasing twice, lead with a link, use numbered/bulleted lists, cite a warranty term or model-year fact you haven't checked.

**Framing that works:** *"had this exact one last week — it's almost always the [X]. Quick check: [one thing]. If that's it, it's a 20-min fix. I wrote up the full thing here if you want the torque specs: [link]"*

## 3. Guest posts (one strong followed link each)
Pitch EV blogs, indie car sites, and **Lithuanian/Baltic auto & EV outlets** (local angle = easy yes, low competition).

**Pitch template:**
> Subject: Guest post — Tesla winter repairs from a Baltic workshop
>
> Hi [name], I'm [name], an independent Tesla mechanic in Kaunas. I fix Model 3/Y/S/X daily and see failure patterns most owners never hear about — especially cold-climate ones. I'd like to write an original, no-fluff piece for [site]: e.g. "5 Tesla failures we see every Baltic winter (and what they cost to fix)." All from real workshop experience, with photos. Exclusive to you. Interested?

## 4. Your linkable asset (the big one — build once, earns links for years)
People link to **original data**. You have data nobody else does: what actually breaks on Teslas in a working Baltic shop, and what it really costs. Publish one authoritative page:

**Working title:** "Real Tesla Repair Costs & Most Common Failures — 2026 Data From a Baltic Workshop"

**What to gather (from your own jobs — even ~50–100 repairs is enough):**
- Top 10–15 failures you actually see, ranked by frequency
- For each: real cost range (Service Center vs independent vs DIY), typical mileage/age, cold-climate factor
- Model 3 vs Y vs S vs X breakdown if you can
- 3–5 workshop photos

**Why it earns links:** journalists, EV bloggers, and forums cite "a mechanic's real failure data" because it's rare and credible. Pitch it to the journalist sites (#1) and to EV blogs the week it goes live. This one page can become your most-linked URL.

## Weekly cadence (~30–45 min, evenings)
- **2 journalist-request answers** (biggest lever).
- **2 genuine forum answers** (link only if it truly helps).
- **Every ~2 weeks:** 1 guest-post pitch, or progress on the data asset.

## How to measure (monthly, via `npm run gsc`)
- **Referring domains** is the number that matters — it's the leading indicator. Rankings follow it by weeks.
- Watch the 🎯 striking-distance queries in the new per-page report: as authority rises, those page-8–15 positions should climb toward page 1, and that's when clicks jump.
- Don't judge by revenue for the first ~3 months. Judge by links earned + positions on the flagged queries.

## What NOT to do
- ❌ Buy links / use PBNs / "guest post networks" — Google penalties, not worth it.
- ❌ Copy-paste the same comment or answer around — reads as spam/AI, which already bit you once.
- ❌ Mass-drop your link in forums without answering the actual question.
