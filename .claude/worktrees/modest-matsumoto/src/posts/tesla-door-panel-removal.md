Removing a door panel is the gateway repair for a lot of Tesla interior work. Speaker upgrades, window regulator replacement, door handle fixes, wiring repairs, sound deadening — all of them start with getting the door panel off.

The good news: Tesla door panels are held on by a handful of T30 Torx screws and plastic push clips. No hidden mystery fasteners, no special Tesla tooling. If you can turn a ratchet and pry gently with a plastic tool, you can do this.

This guide covers all four doors across the Model 3 (2017-2023), Model 3 Highland (2024+), and Model Y (2020-2024), with the specific differences called out for each.

## Before You Start

A few things to know:

- **Buy spare clips.** Every door panel removal has a chance of cracking a clip or two, especially on cold days when plastic gets brittle. A pack of replacement push clips costs a few dollars and saves you from panel rattles later.
- **Use plastic tools only.** Metal screwdrivers and pry bars will scratch your door panel, gouge the paint on the door frame, and crack clip heads. Get a basic trim removal tool set.
- **Work in a warm space if possible.** Below about 5°C (40°F), the plastic clips become stiff and snap more easily.
- **Photograph everything.** Before you disconnect any connector or cable, snap a photo. This is especially important for the Highland Model 3, which has more individual connectors than the older models.

## Tools Needed

- **T30 Torx socket** — the only fastener type on the door panel
- **4-inch ratchet extension** — to reach recessed screws
- **1/4-inch ratchet** (and torque wrench for reinstallation — 6 Nm)
- **Plastic trim removal tools** — flat pry, forked clip remover
- **90° pick tool** — helpful for releasing stubborn push clip retainers from the door
- **Painter's tape** — protect the door frame edges from scratches

## Torque Specification

All Tesla door panels across Model 3 and Model Y share the same spec:

| Fastener | Torque | Tool |
|----------|--------|------|
| Door panel screws (T30 Torx) | 6 Nm (4.4 lb-ft) | T30 Torx socket |

This is verified directly from the Tesla service manual. It applies to all four doors on all covered models.

## Model 3 (2017-2023) — Front Door

This is the most common version you'll encounter. The procedure is the same for Model Y (2020-2024) front doors.

### Removal

1. **Open the door and fully lower the window.** If the window is broken (and that's why you're here), that's fine — just be aware you'll need to support the glass or tape it in place if it's loose.

2. **Remove the front door tweeter.** The small triangular speaker grille at the top front corner of the door pops out with a trim tool. Disconnect the tweeter connector behind it.

3. **Remove the puddle light.** Pop the puddle light lens out of the bottom of the door panel using a trim tool. Disconnect its connector.

4. **Remove the reflector and screw cover.** On pre-2024 Model 3 and Model Y, release the clips holding the reflector to the rear edge of the door panel. Also remove the screw cover at the front of the armrest. Both pop off with a plastic pry tool.

5. **Remove the T30 Torx screws.** There are three screws in three locations — rear, middle, and front. They're now exposed after removing the reflector and screw cover.

6. **Release the clips starting from the puddle light opening.** Insert your trim tool into the puddle light opening at the bottom of the panel and pull outward to pop the first clips. **Do not pull the panel away from the door yet** — there are cables and connectors still attached behind it.

7. **Work the clips from bottom to top.** Continue popping clips around the perimeter of the panel, always working upward. The clips are spaced every few inches around the panel edge.

8. **Raise the panel to unhook it from the door.** Once all clips are released, the panel is held at the top by three tabs that hook into the upper edge of the door. Lift straight up to unhook them. **Stop** — don't pull the panel away yet.

9. **Lift the NVH pad.** The noise-dampening pad on the back of the panel covers the working area. Lift it out of the way.

10. **Disconnect the manual release cable.** Remove the cable collar from the trim panel and pivot it down to vertical. Then slide the cable barrel end out of the release lever. This is the emergency door release mechanism — take note of how it's routed for reinstallation.

11. **Disconnect the electrical harness.** Unplug the connectors from the trim panel and release the clips and hooks holding the harness to the panel. On pre-2024 models, there are typically 2-3 connectors bundled together.

12. **Remove any cable ties** attaching the harness to the panel (if present). On some production runs, you'll find cable ties instead of hooks — cut them and replace with new ones during reinstallation.

13. **Lift the panel away.** Place it face-down on a soft surface (blanket or towel) to protect the finish.

14. **Check for remaining push clip retainers on the door.** Some clips will stay in the door rather than coming out with the panel. Pull them out with a trim tool or 90° pick so they don't interfere with reinstallation.

### Reinstallation

Reverse the removal steps, with these specific notes:

1. **Route the harness into the panel first.** Clip or tie-wrap the harness into its hooks on the back of the panel before offering the panel up to the door.

2. **Connect all electrical connectors** before positioning the panel on the door. Verify the puddle lamp and map pocket ambient light work by turning them on briefly.

3. **Slide the manual release cable barrel into the release lever** with the collar in vertical orientation, then pivot the collar horizontal and press it into the panel. Make sure the cable routes underneath the door harness.

4. **Hook the three panel tabs** into the top edge of the door. Compress the inner belt seal slightly to fit the tabs over the door edge.

5. **Press the clips home** around the panel perimeter. You should hear each one click into place. Check for any gaps — a gap means a misaligned or bent clip.

6. **Reinstall the screws** and torque to 6 Nm (4.4 lb-ft).

7. **Reinstall the reflector, screw cover, puddle light, and tweeter.**

8. **Test the manual door release lever.** Close the door and pull the interior release to confirm it unlatches smoothly. This is a safety check — don't skip it.

9. **Raise the window and close the door.**

**Fastener note:** Tesla specifies particular screw part numbers for these panels. If any screws are damaged or stripped, the replacement PNs are 1488028-00-A or 1526998-99-A (or later revisions). For Model Y, confirm M6-1.0x28 bolt size before installing replacement screws.

## Model 3 Highland (2024+) — Front Door

The Highland refresh changed several things about the door panel. The clip and screw pattern is similar, but the wiring is different — there are more individual connectors to deal with, and the harness routing uses hooks and fir tree clips rather than cable ties.

### Key Differences from Pre-2024

- **Three T30 screws** (same torque: 6 Nm), but the screw locations shifted slightly.
- **No separate screw cover on the armrest** — only the rear reflector needs removal before the screws.
- **More individual electrical connectors.** Instead of a bundled harness with 2-3 connectors, the Highland has five separate connectors to disconnect from the panel: electric release switch, window switch, door LED, atmosphere light, and speaker.
- **Harness secured by hooks (x9) and fir tree clips (x2)** on the back of the panel — no cable ties.
- **NVH pad has four tabs** that snap in and out.
- **Manual release cable routing** includes a cable bracket that the cable slots into before the collar seats in the panel.
- **Service Mode step after reinstallation.** If you're replacing the panel (not just removing and reinstalling the same one), Tesla's procedure calls for running the "Address RGB Lights" routine via the touchscreen (Service Mode > Low Voltage > Interior Lighting > Address RGB Lights). This recalibrates the ambient lighting for the new panel. You need firmware 2024.44 or later.

### Removal

1. **Open the door and fully lower the window.**

2. **Remove the reflector** from the rear edge of the door trim panel.

3. **Remove the three T30 Torx screws.**

4. **Remove the front door tweeter.** Same as older models — pop it out and disconnect.

5. **Remove the puddle light.** Pop it out and disconnect.

6. **Release clips from the puddle light opening, working bottom up.** Pull outward from the puddle light opening to start, then work around the perimeter upward. Tesla recommends using an inflatable air bag at the lower-left corner to help separate the panel if needed.

7. **Release the manual release cable.** First release the cable from its bracket, then remove the cable collar from the trim panel, pivot the collar down to vertical, and slide the barrel end out of the lever.

8. **Disconnect the door harness connector** from the door.

9. **Lift the panel up to unhook the three tabs** from the door edge, and remove the panel.

10. **Remove remaining push clip retainers** from the door. Use a 90° pick — insert it into the retainer and pry inward to break the tabs, then use a trim tool to pop the retainer out.

11. **Remove the NVH pad** from the back of the panel (release four tabs).

12. **Disconnect the five individual connectors:** electric release switch, window switch, door LED, atmosphere light, and speaker. A miniature 90° pick tool makes releasing these connector tabs much easier.

13. **Release the harness from the nine hooks and two fir tree clips**, then lift the harness off the panel.

### Reinstallation

Reverse the removal, noting:

- **Route the harness first** — secure it into all nine hooks and two fir tree clips on the back of the panel before anything else.
- **Connect all five individual connectors** before offering the panel to the door.
- **NVH pad goes in before panel goes on the door** — snap the four tabs.
- **Cable routing:** the manual release cable must route underneath the harness. Slot the barrel into the lever, pivot the collar horizontal, press it into the panel, then secure the cable to its bracket.
- **If replacing the panel** (not just reinstalling the original), run the ambient light calibration routine after reassembly.

## Rear Door Panels — All Models

Rear doors are simpler. The procedure follows the same pattern but with fewer components.

### What's Different on Rear Doors

- **Fewer connectors** — rear doors typically have a puddle light, ambient light, and speaker connector. No tweeter on most configurations, no driver window master switch.
- **Same T30 Torx screws, same 6 Nm torque.**
- **Same clip pattern** — bottom-up release, three tabs hooking into the top edge.
- **Same manual release cable** procedure.
- **Reflector clips on the rear edge** of the panel, same as front.

The rear panel removal is often a 10-15 minute job even on a first attempt. If you're new to this, starting with a rear door is a good way to build confidence before tackling the front.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Pulling the panel away before disconnecting the cable and harness.** The manual release cable and electrical connectors are still attached after the clips release. Yanking the panel away will damage connectors or stretch the cable. Lift the panel just enough to reach behind it.

❌ **Using metal tools on clip edges.** A flathead screwdriver will crack clip heads and gouge the soft plastic panel surface. Plastic pry tools flex and distribute force — that's the point.

❌ **Forgetting to test the manual door release.** After reinstallation, close the door and pull the interior release handle. If the cable wasn't routed correctly, the release won't work — and that's a safety issue in an emergency.

❌ **Not checking for stuck clip retainers on the door.** If push clip retainers stay in the door after panel removal, they'll prevent the new clips from seating properly. The panel will rattle or have visible gaps.

❌ **Over-torquing the T30 screws.** 6 Nm is light — about what you'd use to snug a bicycle handlebar bolt. The screw holes are tapped into the door's metal structure, but the plastic panel can crack around the screw boss if you go too tight.

❌ **Working in freezing temperatures.** Cold plastic cracks. If your garage is below 5°C, bring a space heater or do this on a warmer day.

## Tips from the Shop

A few things we've learned from doing this repeatedly at the shop:

- **Mark your clips.** If you're removing panels on multiple doors, use painter's tape with door labels (FL, FR, RL, RR) to keep clips organized. Clip sizes can vary slightly between doors and model years.
- **Keep a magnet tray handy.** Three small Torx screws per door — that's twelve screws if you're doing all four doors. They roll and disappear.
- **The puddle light opening is your friend.** That's where you start your pry, and it gives you the leverage angle to pop the first row of clips cleanly.
- **First-timers: take a video.** Before you touch anything, do a quick phone video of the panel, connectors, and cable routing. It's better than any photo for figuring out reassembly.
- **Highland owners: label the five connectors.** A piece of masking tape on each connector with a number (1-5 from top to bottom) makes reconnection foolproof.

## Final Thoughts

Door panel removal is one of those repairs that's harder to read about than it is to actually do. Once you pop that first clip, the rest follows a pattern — clips release, panel lifts off, cables and connectors disconnect. The whole thing takes 15-30 minutes on your first try and gets faster every time.

The biggest risk is broken clips, and the fix for that is keeping spare clips on hand. Everything else is straightforward mechanical work with no special tools or skills required.

If you're planning a speaker upgrade, sound deadening project, or window regulator replacement, don't pay a shop for "door panel R&R" labor. This is firmly in DIY territory.
